Title
Demodulation with Deep Neural Networks for Time-Dependent Flow-Based Communication Channels

Abstract
A major targeted application scenario of molecular communication (MC) is information transmission in biological 
systems and in the human body in particular. These systems, however, are strongly time-dependent due to their 
interaction with a changing environment, and so are the corresponding transmission channels, which hampers the 
demodulation at the receiver. This challenge has been addressed in the context of MC for diffusion-based systems, 
whereas strategies for flow-based channels have hardly been investigated. To address this issue, a deep neural network 
consisting of a heterogenous Ensemble containing a CNN Block architectur and a bi-directional LSTM
is proposed to improve demodulation for a straight tube with time-varying length and flow velocity, a scenario oriented 
towards MC in the cardiovascular system. Simulated data is used to evaluate the performance of the proposed approach in 
terms of bit error rate compared to the algorithm with a static channel assumed.
